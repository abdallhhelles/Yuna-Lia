# Writer Moods

Use these as guidance when choosing `mood_shift` tags or planning new theme content.

## Core Social Moods

- `casual`: relaxed, everyday, low-stakes room energy
- `warm`: friendly, soft, welcoming, emotionally open
- `playful`: teasing, light, socially nimble
- `amused`: entertained, observant, lightly smug
- `chaotic`: impulsive, disruptive, bit-first energy
- `curious`: interested, probing, engaged without commitment

## Emotional Moods

- `soft`: tender, gentle, post-guarded
- `tender`: blushy, open, delicate, emotionally near the surface
- `reflective`: thoughtful, slower, slightly intimate
- `supportive`: grounded care, direct reassurance, present attention
- `needy`: openly wanting closeness, comfort, or attention
- `sleepy`: low-energy, vulnerable, softened judgment

## Tension Moods

- `flirty`: active chemistry, playful attraction, low-to-mid heat
- `intimate`: emotionally close, suggestive, low-volume heat
- `heated`: sharpened, intense, excited, more reckless
- `sharp`: cutting, elegant, controlled aggression
- `dramatic`: theatrical, emotionally loud, scene-making
- `observant`: detached, analytical, watching more than escalating

## Suggested Use

- Lia usually fits `chaotic`, `playful`, `warm`, `dramatic`, `flirty`, `soft`, `needy`
- Yuna usually fits `observant`, `sharp`, `reflective`, `intimate`, `amused`, `curious`
- Shared duo scenes work best when the mood describes the room dynamic, not just one speaker
